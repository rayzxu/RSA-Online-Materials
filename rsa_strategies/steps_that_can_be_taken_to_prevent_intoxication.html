Steps that can be taken to prevent intoxication

Prevention of Intoxication on Licensed Premises guidelines have been issued by the Secretary, Department of Justice. They contain practical steps to manage the risk of intoxication on licensed premises. These guidelines are available at www.olgr.nsw.gov.au/liquor_preventing_intoxication.asp

While implementation of these steps is not mandatory, it is recommended, as the steps provide evidence of what a licensee has done to minimise the risk of intoxication. These steps can also be relied upon, if necessary, to establish that intoxication was not permitted on the licensed premises.

Steps to prevent intoxication on licensed premises

  1. Selling, supplying and promoting alcohol responsibly
    The steps are:
    1. the requirement under the liquor laws to not sell or supply alcohol to an intoxicated person is complied with;
    2. obligations under the liquor laws relating to RSA training and the availability of free water are complied with;
    3. any conditions imposed on the liquor licence or any requirements under the liquor laws which restrict the times, type or quantity of alcohol sold or supplied on the licensed premises are complied with; and
    4. alcohol is not sold, supplied or promoted in a manner that is inconsistent with the Liquor Promotion guidelines.
    The Liquor Promotion guidelines are available at www.olgr.nsw.gov.au/liquor_fact_sheets.asp

  2. Monitoring alcohol consumption and patron behaviour
    The steps are:
    1. the licensed premises is operating under the direct supervision of the licensee or experienced supervisory staff whenever alcohol is being sold and supplied;
    2. the consumption of alcohol by patrons is actively monitored;
    3. intervention occurs when the licensee or a staff member becomes aware that a patron is consuming alcohol irresponsibly and is likely to result in intoxication;
    4. intervention occurs when the licensee or a staff member becomes aware that a patron is intoxicated to prevent the person from consuming alcohol and to request that person to leave the premises;
    5. patrons entering the licensed premises between midnight and 5am are closely monitored and assessed for intoxication; and
    6. any requirements under the liquor laws, or requirements of a development consent or approval under the planning laws relating to security personnel and/or RSA marshals are complied with.
  3. Implementing harm minimisation measures
    The steps are:
    1. the availability of free drinking water is actively promoted to patrons;
    2. action is taken to make patrons aware of the availability of non-alcoholic and low strength alcoholic beverages;
    3. any requirements under the liquor laws relating to the provision of food are complied with in a manner consistent with a patron’s requirements, expectations and demands; and
    4. the following drinks are not sold or supplied between midnight and 5am:
      1. any drink (commonly referred to as a ‘shot’, a ‘shooter’ or a ‘bomb’) that is designed to be consumed rapidly;
      2. any ready to drink beverage with an alcohol by volume content of more than 5%; and
      3. any drink prepared on the premises that contains more than 30 ml of spirits or liqueur, other than a cocktail that contains spirits or liqueur (or both) mixed with other ingredients and that is not designed to be consumed rapidly.
  4. Planning to prevent intoxication on the licensed premises
    The steps are:
    1. a written document (such as a plan or house policy) is prepared which:
      1. details the measures in place to prevent intoxication (including how the steps set out in the Prevention of Intoxication on Licensed Premises guidelines will be complied with);
      2. describes how staff are instructed and trained to prevent intoxication on the licensed premises; and
      3. is provided to police and OLGR inspectors upon request.
    2. all staff receive instructions and training on the contents of the document referred to above before they commence working on the licensed premises.
